## What changed and why nothing moved (yet)

v0.6.6 swapped two hot-path lookups from O(N²) to O(N):

- **`tree_find`** in `src/tree.cyr` — was an O(N) linear `streq` walk over the entries vec; now lazily builds a name → entry hashmap on first call per vec, cached by vec pointer for the process lifetime. Hot callers (`cmd_status` × 100, `cmd_diff`, `materialize_target`, `merge` three-way loops) drop from O(N²) to O(N) total. (P-10)
- **`three_way_path_set`** in `src/tree.cyr` — was a nested-`streq` dedup against the growing `paths` vec (~4.5N² streqs for three trees of N entries); now uses a `map_has` membership check. (P-18)

**Why the bench didn't move:** the fixture is 100 files. At that scale, the old O(N²) cost is only ~10000 streqs per status — already under the noise floor compared to the dominant costs (per-file sigil hashing for `status`, per-object zlib for `clone`). The hashmap path adds a small constant overhead (one map build per command) that's also under the noise floor.

The win shows up at scale. Concrete projection for a 1000-file repo:
- Old `cmd_status`: 1000 × tree_find(head_entries) = 1000 × 500 streqs avg = ~500,000 streqs ≈ ~5ms of pure scan.
- New: one map build (1000 inserts) + 1000 lookups (~O(1) each) ≈ ~0.3ms.

For a 10,000-file repo (think large monorepo) the gap is ~50× — the difference between a ~50ms status scan and a ~3ms one. The 100-file synthetic bench can't see this and wasn't meant to.

## What's still unmoved and why

Same story as v0.6.5:

- `status-100files` (6.77 ms): still dominated by 100× SHA-256 hash + 100× file open/read. Filed on [sigil's roadmap](../../../sigil/docs/development/roadmap.md) as the SHA-256 throughput investigation.
- `clone-100commits` (215 ms): still dominated by ~500 source-side SQL ops × ~200µs. The cleanest remaining wins are gated on patra's [1.7.0 `INSERT OR IGNORE`](../../../patra/docs/development/roadmap.md) (drops the 2-SELECT pattern to 1) and patra's [1.8.0 WAL group commit](../../../patra/docs/development/roadmap.md) (auto-batches fsync without explicit transactions).
- `add-1MB` (207 ms): still dominated by zlib_compress(1MB). Filed on [sankoch's roadmap](../../../sankoch/docs/development/roadmap.md).

## Next ship targets

Per [the roadmap](../development/roadmap.md), the remaining sit-side perf items are:

- **P-04** walk-reachable cache — would save ~10ms on `clone` (the walk-decompression piece). Modest absolute win; worth it when bundled with other small items.
- **P-06 + P-15** smarter decompression sizing + LCS DP table through `fl_alloc`. Smaller wins on diff/clone; tightens bump-heap pressure.
- **P-17** buffered stdout. Cosmetic on most commands; ~3000 syscalls collapse for a 500-line diff.

The next significant `clone` improvement is **patra 1.7.0 `INSERT OR IGNORE`** landing — a sit dep bump on that day will drop the inner `db_object_has` and project ~30% on top of v0.6.5/v0.6.6's clone numbers without further sit-side code change.
